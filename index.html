Books for Women

Read these books, and be proud of yourself. As a women.

Non Fiction

Becoming

by. Michelle Obama

In her memoir, a work of deep reflection and mesmerizing storytelling, Michelle Obama invites readers into her world, chronicling the experiences that have shaped her—from her childhood on the South Side of Chicago to her years as an executive balancing the demands of motherhood and work, to her time spent at the world’s most famous address.

I am Malala

by. Malala Yousafzai

I Am Malala is the remarkable tale of a family uprooted by global terrorism, of the fight for girls' education, of a father who, himself a school owner, championed and encouraged his daughter to write and attend school, and of brave parents who have a fierce love for their daughter in a society that prizes sons.

The Blue Book

by. Tarshi

Am I attractive? Am I ‘normal’? Do I even want to be? When is the right time to start having sex? What is sex really all about? How will I know if my relationship is good for me? Written specifically for adolescents, The Blue Book is a guide to exploring sexuality and sexual feelings, as well as a trustworthy fact-finding and fact-checking resource.

Fiction

The Midnight Library

by. Matt Haig

In The Midnight Library, Matt Haig’s enchanting new novel, Nora Seed finds herself faced with this decision. Faced with the possibility of changing her life for a new one, following a different career, undoing old breakups, realizing her dreams of becoming a glaciologist; she must search within herself as she travels through the Midnight Library to decide what is truly fulfilling in life, and what makes it worth living in the first place.

The Shark Caller

by. Zillah Bethell

Desperate to become a shark caller to avenge the death of her parents, Blue Wing is instead charged with befriending infuriating newcomer Maple. At first they are angry and out of sync with the island and each other. But when the tide breathes the promise of treasure, can they overcome their differences and brave the deadliest shark in the ocean?
